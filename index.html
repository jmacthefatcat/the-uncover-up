Personal Bio -

Nathan Radke is an author, professor, researcher, and podcast host based out of Toronto, Ontario, Canada. After growing up in a house filled with Time-Life "Mysteries of the Unknown" books he developed a thirst for learning about those things that are right on the edge of our understanding, and now uses his training in logic and social theory to try to comprehend our viral information ecosystem. He currently lives in a cement bunker/recording studio, and spends much of his time pouring over redacted files and filling out "Freedom of Information" requests.

Personal Bio -

Lee Kuhnle is a college professor in the Faculty of Liberal Arts & Sciences at Humber College, a co-host of The Uncover Up podcast as well as a regular contributor to the Conspiracy Roundtable radio show on CFRB1010. He is a graduate of York University’s social and political thought doctoral program. Lee researches broadly in the field of ‘ideology critique,’ focusing most recently on the role of conspiracy theories in liberal democratic political cultures. Appearance supported by Humber College Liberal Arts & Sciences.

Alena CLASSIFIED Papayanis Favourite Cryptid -Human Beings Personal Conspiracy Obsession -Death of Frank Olsen Household Cryptid -Dog with two different coloured eyes
Personal Bio -

Alena Papayanis is a Professor of Arts and Humanities at Humber College, co-host of The Uncover Up podcast, Tiktok content creator, and writer. Her academic studies have been interdisciplinary, with a PhD in Cultural History from the University of London, Birkbeck College and a B.A. and M.A. Media and Cultural studies from the University of Western Ontario. Her postgraduate research has focused on the contemporary cultural history of war within the United States, and her more recent research examines privilege and oppression in post-secondary education, as well as conspiracy theories in popular culture.

Sean Egerton Foreman is a filmmaker and digital producer based out of Toronto, Ontario, Canada. Along with creating video content for The UncoverUp, Sean also produces work for his own video series Mystery Club. A mix between Unsolved Mysteries and Monty Python, Mystery Club uses humour and animation to explore some of the greatest mysteries of all time. Sean is drawn to work that not only intrigues, but informs. His work has been featured on CBC, Rolling Stone, and Much Music. To view more of Sean’s work please visit www.seanegertonforeman.com.
